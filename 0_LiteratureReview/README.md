**Summary of Each Work**:

- **Source 1**: Baird, Engberg & Gutierrez (2022): RCT evidence on differential impact of US job training programmes by pre-training employment status

  - **[Link]()** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0927537122000331?via%3Dihub
  - **Objective**: What is the impact of New Orleans' job training program "Career Pathways" on employment, earnings,
and industry of employment of unemployed or underemployed people between 2014, the year of introduction, and 2019?
  - **Methods**:
  -   Program outcomes:
  -     Regression with fixed effects
  -   Labor outcomes:
  -     Intent-to-treat (ITT) model estimated using OLS regression and fixed effects
  -     Local average treatment (LATE) effect model estimated by 2SLS regression (treatment assignment as instrument)
  - **Outcomes**:
  -   Program outcomes: Insignificant effect on average, and no significant differences between groups is likelihood of attending and completing trainings 1 and 2, but the long-term unemployed were significantly more likely to attend and complete training 2 than other groups.
  -   Labor outcomes: Likelihood of being employed after the training only increased significantly for the short-term unemployed (by 14pp COMPARED TO CONTROl, long-term unemployed?). Quarterly earnings increased by 12% (ITT) or 20% (LATE) on average, respectively, compared to the control group. Again, the short-term unemployed benefitted the most.
  - **Relation to the Project**: This study has a very similar setting to my study since it is also based on an RCT.
